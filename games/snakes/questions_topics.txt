Level 0

Say Hello! (Intro): Basic introductions (Hello! What’s your name? I’m …), colours, numbers 1–10. No major new grammar.
Unit 1: My classroom: Imperatives (e.g., Look!, Sit down.); What is it? It’s …
Unit 2: My body: I’ve got … (have got for possession); How many … ? questions.
Unit 3: My family: This is my … ; Is this your … ? (demonstratives and yes/no questions).
Unit 4: At the zoo: Adjectives (descriptive); What’s your favourite … ?
Unit 5: My food: I like / I don’t like … ; Do you like … ? (preferences and questions).
Unit 6: My town: There’s / There are … ; Where’s the … ? It’s there. (existence and location).
Unit 7: Jobs: He’s / She’s a … ; Is he/she a … ? (present simple descriptions and questions).
Unit 8: My clothes: I’m wearing … (present continuous); Is he/she wearing … ?
Unit 9: My hobbies: I’m + -ing (present continuous actions); Are you + -ing ? questions.

Level 1

Friends (Intro): Basic introductions and greetings (What’s your name? I’m …; How old are you?); possessives with colours/names.
Unit 1: At school: Questions and short answers (What’s this? Is it a …? Yes, it is./No, it isn’t.); Imperatives.
Unit 2: Let's play: Possessives (What’s his/her … ?); How old is he/she?; Adjectives.
Unit 3: Pet show: Prepositions of place (in, on, under); I like / I don’t like … (with animals/toys).
Unit 4: Lunchtime: I’ve got / I haven’t got … ; Have … got any … ? (have got questions).
Unit 5: Free time: Present simple with days (I … on Sundays.); Do you … ? Yes, I do./No, I don’t.
Unit 6: The old house: There’s / There are … ; Is/Are there … ?; How many … ?
Unit 7–9 (and remaining units): Build on present simple, prepositions, can for ability, and basic questions (exact points follow similar progression to Level 2 patterns, focusing on routines, abilities, and descriptions).

Level 2

Back to school (Intro): There’s a … / There are some … ; Imperatives.
Unit 1: My day: Telling the time; Present simple (3rd person singular: gets up, goes …).
Unit 2: The zoo: Likes / doesn’t like; Does … like … ? Yes, he/she does./No, he/she doesn’t.
Unit 3: Where we live: Has … got … ?; Prepositions (of place).
Unit 4: The market: Would you like … ?; Is there/Are there any … ? (some/any with countable/uncountable).
Unit 5: My bedroom: This/that/these/those; Whose … is this/are these?
Unit 6: People: Am/Is/Are + adjective; our/their (possessives); months.
Unit 7: Off we go!: I’d like to … ; Questions with verb + -ing.
Unit 8: Sports club: -ing forms (gerunds); like + -ing.
Unit 9: Holiday plans: Can for requests/ability.

Level 3

Meet The Explorers (Intro): Be good at + -ing; Possessive apostrophe ('s).
Unit 1: Our school: Like / Don’t like + -ing; Have to / has to + infinitive.
Unit 2: The picnic: Questions and answers with some and any.
Unit 3: Daily tasks: Telling the time; Adverbs of time/frequency.
Unit 4: Around town: Prepositions; Be going to + infinitive (of purpose).
Unit 5: Under the sea: Was / Were; Questions and answers with was/were (past of be).
Unit 6: Gadgets: Comparatives; Superlatives.
Unit 7: In the hospital: Past simple regular verbs; Past simple irregular verbs.
Unit 8: Around the world: Negatives with past simple; Past simple questions and answers.
Unit 9: Holiday plans: Future with be going to + infinitive; Questions and answers with be going to
